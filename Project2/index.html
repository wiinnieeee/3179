WORLD DISASTER RISK

From 2010 to 2021

Disasters are sudden events that always cause widespread destruction, major damage or loss of life, either done by forces of nature or human beings. Disasters happen frequently across the globe, from small sovereigns to major countries. In WorldRiskReport, several indicators are used to determine the disaster risks for countries and regions worldwide. The higher the World Risk Index (WRI) of the country, the higher its national disaster risks.

#1 GLOBAL OVERVIEW

Through this map, we can see the WRI category of the regions worldwide in a big picture. We discover that throughout the years, South East Asian and African countries always have medium, high and very high WRI. A noticable change is Uruguay, in which the WRI shot up from low to very high within a year in 2019. This may because of the floods and earthquakes happening more frequently starting from 2019 in Uruguay.





#2 STATS

The WRI are accessed from several attributes, such as Exposure, Vulnerability, Susceptibility, Lack of Adaptive Capacities and Lack of Coping Capabilities . From the idiom below, we are able to observe the correlation of the attributes with each other and also with the WRI. Selecting from the years, we can filter the pattern for each year as well. Overall, one obvious observation we can make is that most of the attributes have a positive correlation between them. The positive correlation between the WRI and exposure would be highlighted in one of the idioms below in detail as it is most related with WRI from the scatterplot matrix.





#3 TOP-N REGIONS

For each year, we could rank the top-N most dangerous regions, a.k.a those with highest WRI, in which N is the number of regions. Within the 10 years time, Vanuatu led at the first place, making it the most dangerous region in the world.




#4 TREND OF ATTRIBUTES

Looking at more details on the attribute but focusing more on the big picture of the regions, in which we classify them into 5 major continents. Except for exposure in which Oceania has the highest index score, Africas top the chart for the other attributes. We can say that European countries are the safest among all the other continents since the index score for all attributes are the lowest throughout the 10 years compared to other continents.
